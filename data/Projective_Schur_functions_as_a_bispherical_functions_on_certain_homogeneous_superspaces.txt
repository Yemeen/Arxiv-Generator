                                              PROJECTIVE SCHUR FUNCTIONS AS A BISPHERICAL FUNCTIONS
                                                       ON CERTAIN HOMOGENEOUS SUPERSPACES

                                                                                     ALEXANDER SERGEEV
arXiv:math/0106233v1 [math.RT] 27 Jun 2001




                                                    Abstract. I show that the projective Schur functions may be interpreted as bispherical
                                                    functions of either the triple (q(n), q(n)⊕ q(n), q(n)), where q(n) is the “odd” (queer) analog
                                                    of the general linear Lie algebra, or the triple (pe(n), gl(n|n), pe(n)), where pe(n) is the
                                                    periplectic Lie superalgebra which preserves the nondegenerate odd bilinear form (either
                                                    symmetric or skew-symmetric). Making use of this interpretation I characterize projective
                                                    Schur functions as common eigenfunctions of an algebra of differential operators.




                                                                                         Introduction
                                               The ground field is C.

                                             1.1. In [Sch] I. Schur introduced projective Schur functions as characteristics of projective
                                             representations of symmetric groups. In [Se1] I showed that these characteristics are actually
                                             characters of tensor representations of Lie superalgebra q(n).
                                                In [St] Stembridge interpreted projective Schur functions as characteristics of of spherical
                                             functions of a certain twisted Gelfand pair (for definition see [St]). Here I will remind
                                             definition of bispherical functions and demonstrate that these Stembridge’s characteristics
                                             are precisely bispherical functions of the triple (pe(n), gl(n|n), pe(n)), where pe(n) can be
                                             embedded into gl(n|n) in two different ways (corresponding to interpretation of pe(n) as the
                                             algebra preserving a symmetric odd bilinear form or a skew-symmetric one).
                                                One obtains one more realization of projective Schur functions if one considers bispherical
                                             functions of the triple (q(n), q(n) ⊕ q(n), q(n)), where q(n) can be embedded into q(n) ⊕ q(n)
                                             by one of the two ways: either as the diagonal or as a “twisted diagonal”.
                                                Both ways to realize projective Schur functions allow one to construct an algebra of dif-
                                             ferential operators for which the projective Schur functions are eigenfunctions. This algebra
                                             appears as the algebra of radial parts of Laplace operators for the Lie superalgebra gl(n|n)
                                             or q(n).

algebra U(g) possesses a canonical antiautomorphism t : u 7→ t u which extends the principal
antiautomorphism of g given by the formula t(x) = −x for any x ∈ g as follows:

   The left and right coregular representations of U(g) are defined for any l ∈ U(g)∗ and

2.3. Left and right coregular representations. For any u, v ∈ U(g) and L ∈ U(g)∗ set

             (L∗ (u)L)(v) = (−1)uL L(t uv) and (R∗ (u)L)(v) = (−1)u(L+v) L(vu).

The following statements are easy to verify:
  i) u −→ L∗ (u) is a representation of U(g) in U(g)∗ (we call it the left regular one);
  ii) u −→ R∗ (u) is a representation of U(g) in U(g)∗ (we call it the right regular one);
  iii) If x ∈ g, then L∗ (x) and R∗ (x) are superdifferentiations of the algebra U(g)∗ .
Observe also that algebra U(g)∗ possesses an automorphism

                  L 7→ LT : LT (u) = L(t u) for any u ∈ U(g), L ∈ U(g)∗ .
