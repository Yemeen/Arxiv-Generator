                                        1. Introduction
   The aim of this paper is to show that two blocks of symmetric groups with isomorphic defect
groups have equivalent derived categories. We deduce in particular that BroueÌâ€™s abelian defect
group conjecture holds for symmetric groups. We prove similar results for general linear groups
over finite fields and cyclotomic Hecke algebras.
   Recall that there is an action of slË† p on the sum of Grothendieck groups of categories of
kSn -modules, for n â‰¥ 0, where k is a field of characteristic p. The action of the generators
ei and fi come from exact functors between modules (â€œi-inductionâ€ and â€œi-restrictionâ€). The
adjoint action of the simple reflections of the affine Weyl group can be categorified as functors
between derived categories, following Rickard. The key point is to show that these functors are
invertible, since two blocks have isomorphic defect groups if and only if they are in the same
affine Weyl group orbit. This involves only an sl2 -action and we solve the problem in a more
general framework.
   We develop a notion of sl2 -categorification on an abelian category. This involves the data of
adjoint exact functors E and F inducing an sl2 -action on the Grothendieck group and the data
of endomorphisms X of E and T of E 2 satisfying the defining relations of (degenerate) affine
Hecke algebras.
   Our main Theorem is a proof that the categorification Î˜ of the simple reflection is a self-
equivalence at the level of derived (and homotopy) categories. We achieve this in two steps.
First, we show that there is a minimal categorification of string (=simple) modules coming from
certain quotients of (degenerate) affine Hecke algebras : this reduces the proof of invertibility of
Î˜ to the case of the minimal categorification. There, Î˜ becomes (up to shift) a self-equivalence
of the abelian category.
   Let us now describe in more detail the structure of this article. The first part Â§3 is devoted to
the study of (degenerate) affine Hecke algebras of type A completed at a maximal ideal corre-
sponding to a totally ramified central character. We construct (in Â§3.2) explicit decompositions
of tensor products of ideals which we later translate into isomorphisms of functors. In Â§3.3,
we introduce certain quotients, that turn out to be Morita equivalent to cohomology rings of
Grassmannians. Part Â§4 recalls elementary results on adjunctions and on representations of
sl2 .
   Part Â§5 is devoted to the definition and study of sl2 -categorifications. We first define a weak
version (Â§5.1), with functors E and F satisfying sl2 -relations in the Grothendieck group. This
is enough to get filtrations of the category and to introduce a class of objects that control
the abelian category. Then, in Â§5.2, we introduce the extra data of X and T which give the
genuine sl2 -categorifications. This provides actions of (degenerate) affine Hecke algebras on
powers of E and F . This leads immediately to two constructions of divided powers of E and

F . In order to study sl2 -categorifications, we introduce in Â§5.3 â€œminimalâ€ categorifications of
the simple sl2 -representations, based on the quotients introduced in Â§3.3. A key construction
(Â§5.4.2) is a functor from such a minimal categorification to a given categorification, that
allows to reduce part of the study of an arbitrary sl2 -categorification to this minimal case,
where explicit computations can be carried out. This corresponds to the decomposition of
the sl2 -representation on K0 into a direct sum of irreducible representations. We use this in
Â§5.5 to prove a categorified version of the relation [e, f ] = h and deduce a construction of
categorifications on the module category of the endomorphism ring of â€œstableâ€ objects in a
given categorification.
   Part Â§6 is devoted to the categorification of the simple reflection of the Weyl group. In Â§6.1, we
construct a complex of functors categorifying this reflection, following Rickard. The main result
is Theorem 6.4 in part Â§6.2, which shows that this complex induces a self-equivalence of the
homotopy and of the derived category. The key step in the proof for the derived category is the
case of a minimal categorification, where we show that the complex has homology concentrated
in one degree (Â§6.3). The case of the homotopy category is reduced to the derived category
thanks to the constructions of Â§5.5.
   In part Â§7, we study various examples. We define (in Â§7.1) sl2 -categorifications on represen-
tations of symmetric groups and deduce derived and even splendid Rickard equivalences. We
deduce a proof of BroueÌâ€™s abelian defect group conjecture for blocks of symmetric groups. We
give similar constructions for cyclotomic Hecke algebras (Â§7.2) and for general linear groups
over a finite field in the non-defining characteristic case (Â§7.3) for which we also deduce the
validity of BroueÌâ€™s abelian defect group conjecture. We also construct sl2 -categorifications on
category O for gln (Â§7.4) and on rational representations of GLn over an algebraically closed
field of characteristic p > 0 (Â§7.5). This answers in particular the GL case of a conjecture
of Rickard on blocks corresponding to weights with same stabilizers under the dot action of
the affine Weyl group. We also explain similar constructions for q-Schur algebras (Â§7.6) and
provide morphisms of categorifications relating the previous constructions. A special role is
played by the endomorphism X, which takes various incarnations : the Casimir in the rational
representation case and the Jucys-Murphy elements in the Hecke algebra case. In the case of
the general linear groups over a finite field, our construction seems to be new. Our last section
(Â§7.7) provides various realizations of minimal categorifications, including one coming from the
geometry of Grassmannian varieties.
