                                                      IN SEVERAL DISCRETE INDETERMINATES

                                                                                           A. SERGEEV
arXiv:math/0202182v1 [math.RT] 18 Feb 2002




                                                    Abstract. Let A be an associative algebra over C and L an invariant linear functional
                                                    on it (trace). Let ω be an involutive antiautomorphism of A such that L(ω(a)) = L(a) for
                                                    any a ∈ A. Then A admits a symmetric invariant bilinear form ha, bi = L(aω(b)). For
                                                    A = U (sl(2))/m, where m is any maximal ideal of U (sl(2)), Leites and I have constructed
                                                    orthogonal basis whose elements turned out to be, essentially, Chebyshev and Hahn poly-
                                                    nomials in one discrete variable.
                                                       Here I take A = U (gl(3))/m for the maximal ideals m which annihilate irreducible highest
                                                    weight gl(3)-modules of particular form (generalizations of symmetric powers of the identity
                                                    representation). In whis way we obtain multivariable analogs of Hahn polynomials.

1.5.1. Twisted generalized Weyl algebras. Recall definition of twisted generalized Weyl
algebras introduced in [MT]. Let R be a commutative algebra over C, Γ a finite nonoriented
tree with Γ0 being the set of its vertices and Γ1 that of edges. Let also {σi : i ∈ Γ0 } be a
set of pairwise commuting automorphisms of R and {ti : i ∈ Γ0 } the set of nonzero elements
from R satisfying the following conditions:
                                  ti tj = σi−1 (tj )σj−1 (ti ) if (i, j) ∈ Γ1 ,
                                                                                                    (1.5.1)
                                  σi (tj ) = tj if (i, j) 6∈ Γ1 .

Lemma . ([MT]) Let A′ be the algebra generated by Xi , Yi : i ∈ Γ0 subject to the relations
(for any r ∈ R)
   1) Xi r = σi (r)Xi ,
   2) Yi r = σi−1 (r)Yi ,
   3) Xi Yj = Yj Xi if i 6= j,
   4) Yi Xi = ti ,
   5) Xi Yi = σi (ti ).
   Then A′ 6= 0, A′ is Z-graded, and among homogeneous (with respect to the grading)
twosided ideals of A′ whose intersection with R is trivial is a maximal one, I.
    The quotient A = A′ /I is called the twisted generalized Weyl algebra.
1.5.2. Example. Let γ be the Dynkin graph for the root system An−1 . Let V be an n-
dimensional vector space with basis e1 , . . . , en , and let ε1 , . . . , εn be the dual basis of V ∗ . Let
T = {εi − εj } be the root system of An−1 in V ∗ and α1 , . . . , αn−1 the system of simple roots.
Set R = S(V ) and for h ∈ V define:
                              σi (h) = h − αi (h) for i = 1, 2, . . . , n − 1
and having extending σi to an automorphism of R. Clearly, the σi pairwise commute for
i ∈ Γ0 . In V , select vectors h1 , . . . , hn−1 such that αi (hj ) = δij and for an arbitrary collection
f1 , . . . , fn−1 of polynomials in one indeterminate set
              t1 (v) = f1 (h1 )f2 (h2 − h1 ),    t2 (v) = f2 (h2 − h1 + 1)f3 (h3 − h2 ), . . . ,
              tn−1 (v) = fn−1 (hn−1 − hn−2 + 1)fn (hn−1 ).
    It is not difficult to verify that conditions (1.5.1) are satisfied.
